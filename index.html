Humanity’s Unburnable Library

What Is Public Domain?

Public domain signifies a work—be it a book, song, or artwork—freed from copyright protection. This occurs when copyright expires, such as in the U.S. where works published before 1929 enter the public domain by 2025. Once there, these treasures belong to all, inviting free use, sharing, and creation without restriction.

What Is Web 3?

Web 3 heralds the internet’s next era, championing decentralization and user sovereignty. Unlike Web 2’s centralized platforms, Web 3 leverages blockchain to distribute data across networks, enhancing security and resisting censorship. For our library, this means a decentralized haven where works endure beyond control.

The Library’s Purpose

We embrace public domain works as a gift to humanity, free from legal bounds, allowing us to preserve and share classics with open hearts. By anchoring them on the Dogecoin blockchain and IPFS, we ensure their eternal accessibility, a beacon against censorship or loss.

IPFS and Dogecoin’s Role

IPFS stores our works across a distributed network, guaranteeing availability even if the internet falters. Dogecoin’s blockchain inscribes their references, forging a permanent, unalterable record. Together, they weave the Unburnable Library into a decentralized tapestry of human heritage.
